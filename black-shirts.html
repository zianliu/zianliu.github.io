Black Shirts

16 May 2013 on Style

I don’t understand the new craze about wearing black shirts with black ties (often with black suits and shoes too). It probably started somewhere in Hollywood, and I guess it looks “fashion forward.” However, menswear color is supposed to mimic or enhance the contrast of one’s natural colors (i.e. hair vs. skin vs. eyes). Black-on-black does little to mimic most skin-hair contrasts, and for those with black skin and black hair, clothing should enhance contrast, and the combination definitely does nothing to enhance contrast. Instead, one would look either like an awkward black blob or an even more awkward black blob with a different-colored tip. By the same logic, black shirts with other colored ties suffer the same problem. Instead of an awkward blob with a colored tip, the ensemble would resemble an awkward blob with a colored strip down the middle – worse, if you ask me.

Moreover, black-on-black makes one look like Il Duce, and if anyone wants to resemble the Fascist dictator of Italy during World War II, I think I’ll have fundamental disagreements with that person.

So, instead of wearing black shirts with any neckwear, I instead recommend wearing them tieless – with slightly contrasting pants (such as darker denim jeans), if possible. Also, the jacket should be forgone if possible. However, I admit that a rougher sport coat with black patterns could look extremely nice with a black shirt.

If you must wear a tie with a black shirt, it is perhaps best to wear a tie with black background but some pattern of a different color. Though the ensemble isn’t ideal, it at least introduces some contrast without too much tackiness. However, if you can avoid it, I strongly ask you to consider wearing something else instead.
