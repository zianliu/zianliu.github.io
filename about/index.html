About Zian

I’m a Master of Translational Medicine candidate at UC Berkeley and UC San Francisco. After my sixteenth continuous year in school, I hope to apply my interdisciplinary training to a career in enabling responsible innovation in biomedicine.

As a graduate researcher at UCSF Neurology, I’m currently working on developing the Open Multiple Sclerosis BioScreen, an open-access digital health platform that uses machine learning to help MS patients contextualize their disease progression. In addition to working on feature development in the backend, I’m also performing market and regulatory analysis for the tool, as well as contributing to its UX design. I’m excited by the project’s potential to democratize and extend the reach of precision medicine.

Prior to starting MTM, in 2016, I received my Bachelor of Science in Bioengineering from the University of California, Berkeley, with High Honors; and completed certificates from the Goldman School of Public Policy and SynBERC. I conducted research in biofuels synthesis and bioweapon defense, and as a part of my senior project, designed and constructed drug-eluting contact lenses for treating corneal infections at a low-resource setting.

Throughout college, I was also active in student organizations, and, upon graduation, received Berkeley’s Bioengineering Department Service Award. I served as the 15th President of the Bioengineering Honor Society and the Vivian Shen Distinguished Chair of Tau Beta Pi. There, I spearheaded projects in educational outreach, networking, cultural awareness, and bridging the gap between STEM and the humanities. I’m particularly passionate about expanding access to STEM education, and co-founded the Bioengineering High School Competition, which, as of 2016, is the only bioengineering design competition for high schoolers in the United States.

In my spare time, I follow both American and international politics, and am a menswear, fountain pen, and coffee enthusiast. I’m always looking for new opportunities, discussions on current affairs, and to give (often unwarranted) style advice. Welcome to my site!
