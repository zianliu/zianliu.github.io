Is There Such a Thing As Too Much Blue?

21 Nov 2016 on Style

I love the color blue - it’s probably my favorite - and I wear it a lot. I own three solid navy suits and three solid navy sportcoats, most of my dress shirts are in shades of blue or are white with blue patterns, a majority of my ties are blue, and of course I have plenty of blue other accessories. Blue is an easy color to incorporate into any business/classic menswear ensemble, but the ensemble I wore to a departmental banquet tonight got me thinking about whether there’s such a thing as “too much blue.”

My ensemble wasn’t all that uncommon - navy suit, medium blue shirt, and navy dotted tie. In fact, President Obama has been wearing darker shirts in his blue-on-blue ensembles recently, creating an overall “tonal” look, but this doesn’t seem to work quite as well on me. I subscribe to the Alan Flusser’s school of thought that the contrast within a person’s clothing should seek to complement their natural contrast, which means that East Asians like me would look best with higher-contrast ensembles to go with the yellow skin and black hair. The suit-shirt-tie combo I wore, however, has very little contrast - practically playing between navy and medium blue. The low-contrast problem is exacerbated by the fact that the only color in play was blue - a cool color known for its tranquil, peaceful connotations. While good as a base for combining with other colors and patters, wearing an ensemble with too much blue, especially multiple pieces of similar hues, can function to wash out the wearer’s other features and make him supposedly “fade away.”

So, while my ensemble didn’t necessarily look “bad,” a lighter shirt, or even a patterned one, could have enhanced it. A tie with a bit more contrast - possibly with broader white stripes rather than the less conspicuous dots - would have also done the trick. I did try to add as much contrast as possible with my pocket square by wearing one with a black and white houndstooth, which I thought would provide both pattern and difference in color, but, looking back, a square with slightly more white probably would have worked better.

Blue in all of its shades are great colors for menswear, but be wary of overusing it. Careful management of contrast is necessary to prevent blue ensembles from fading away.

Zian Liu
